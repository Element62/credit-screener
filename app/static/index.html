    Screening Methodology

    How the Issuer Screen is Built

    Step 1 — Bloomberg SRCH Universe

    Using the SRCH function in Bloomberg, the following filters are applied to generate the initial universe of securities:

    FilterCriteria
    Security TypeBonds, Preferred, and Loans
    StatusActive
    Maturity≥ 2 months from current date
    Mid Price< 90
    Ultimate Parent Amount Outstanding> $500MM
    Issue Size> $200MM
    Country / Region of Incorporation / RiskCanada or United States
    Yield to Maturity> 7%

    Step 2 — Issuer Aggregation

    The resulting bonds, loans, and preferred securities are grouped by their Ultimate Parent Ticker. For each issuer, the dashboard computes:

    • Face ($BN) — total qualifying face split by Secured, Unsecured, and Preferred
    • 52W Peak Upside / Return to Par — dollar upside if prices revert to 52-week highs or par, by seniority
    • Price — face-weighted average price across qualifying instruments
    • Yield — face-weighted average yield across qualifying instruments

    Issuers with no qualifying instruments (after all filters) are excluded from the screen unless they contain defaulted bonds.

    Additional Exclusions

    • Defaulted — defaulted bonds are excluded from issuer-level metrics and shown in a separate table beneath the issuer detail view
    • Subordinated — bonds with subordinated payment rank are excluded (non-preferred only)
